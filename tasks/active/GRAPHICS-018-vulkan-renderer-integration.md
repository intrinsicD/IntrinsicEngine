The aggregate read is not tear-free across fields (each field is loaded with relaxed atomics in a fixed order: bindless, transfer, pipeline-count, last-pipeline-reason); single-threaded CPU contract coverage in `Test.VulkanFailClosedContract.cpp` (`FallbackDiagnosticsSnapshotMatchesIndividualGetters`, `FallbackDiagnosticsSnapshotIsProcessMonotonic`) asserts agreement with the individual getters and process-monotonic delta semantics.
  - `VulkanDevice::BeginFrame` on a non-operational device (or before swapchain bring-up) increments a process-monotonic `GetFallbackBeginFrameAttemptCount()` counter and emits a structured log breadcrumb, mirroring the bindless/transfer/pipeline counter pattern. The counter is also exposed as `FallbackDiagnosticsSnapshot::BeginFrameAttempts` (loaded last in the snapshot field order: bindless, transfer, pipeline-count, last-pipeline-reason, begin-frame-count). CPU coverage: `VulkanFailClosedContract.BeginFrameOnNonOperationalDeviceIncrementsAttemptCounter` asserts the counter increments per call; `FallbackCountersAreProcessMonotonicAcrossInitializeShutdownCycles`, `FallbackDiagnosticsSnapshotMatchesIndividualGetters`, and `FallbackDiagnosticsSnapshotIsProcessMonotonic` extend to cover the new field across init/shutdown and through the snapshot accessor. This catches accidental runtime/renderer frame loops driving a fail-closed Vulkan device on CPU CI.
  - `VulkanDevice::EndFrame` on a non-operational device increments a process-monotonic `GetFallbackEndFrameAttemptCount()` counter and emits a structured log breadcrumb, mirroring the bindless/transfer/pipeline/begin-frame counter pattern. The counter is also exposed as `FallbackDiagnosticsSnapshot::EndFrameAttempts`, appended after `BeginFrameAttempts` in the snapshot field order (bindless, transfer, pipeline-count, last-pipeline-reason, begin-frame-count, end-frame-count). CPU coverage: `VulkanFailClosedContract.EndFrameOnNonOperationalDeviceIncrementsAttemptCounter` asserts the counter increments per call; `BeginEndFrameCountersTrackPairwiseOnNonOperationalDevice` asserts that paired `BeginFrame`/`EndFrame` calls advance both counters in lockstep, locking in the frame-loop pair invariant; `FallbackCountersAreProcessMonotonicAcrossInitializeShutdownCycles`, `FallbackDiagnosticsSnapshotMatchesIndividualGetters`, and `FallbackDiagnosticsSnapshotIsProcessMonotonic` extend to cover the new field across init/shutdown and through the snapshot accessor.
  - `VulkanDevice::Present` on a non-operational device or before swapchain bring-up increments a process-monotonic `GetFallbackPresentAttemptCount()` counter and emits a structured log breadcrumb. The counter is exposed as `FallbackDiagnosticsSnapshot::PresentAttempts`, appended after `EndFrameAttempts` in the snapshot field order (bindless, transfer, pipeline-count, last-pipeline-reason, begin-frame-count, end-frame-count, present-count). CPU coverage: `VulkanFailClosedContract.PresentOnNonOperationalDeviceIncrementsAttemptCounter` asserts per-call increments; `BeginEndPresentCountersTrackLifecycleOnNonOperationalDevice` asserts that a fail-closed Begin/End/Present loop advances all three lifecycle counters in lockstep; the existing process-monotonic and snapshot tests extend to cover the field across init/shutdown and through the aggregate accessor. Together with `BeginFrameAttempts` and `EndFrameAttempts`, this completes CPU-visible lifecycle diagnostics for fail-closed renderer/runtime frame loops driving Vulkan presentation.
  - `VulkanDevice::Resize` on a non-operational device or before swapchain bring-up increments a process-monotonic `GetFallbackResizeAttemptCount()` counter, emits a structured log breadcrumb, and still records the requested extent for CPU/runtime diagnostics. The counter is exposed as `FallbackDiagnosticsSnapshot::ResizeAttempts`, appended after `PresentAttempts` in the snapshot field order (bindless, transfer, pipeline-count, last-pipeline-reason, begin-frame-count, end-frame-count, present-count, resize-count). CPU coverage: `VulkanFailClosedContract.ResizeOnNonOperationalDeviceIncrementsAttemptCounterAndRecordsExtent` asserts per-call increments and pending extent state; the existing process-monotonic and snapshot tests extend to cover the field across init/shutdown and through the aggregate accessor. Operational resize/swapchain-recreation diagnostics remain a bring-up blocker.
  - `GetVulkanFrameLifecycleDiagnosticsSnapshot()` now exposes a backend-local structured lifecycle taxonomy for the most recent promoted Vulkan `BeginFrame`, `EndFrame`, `Present`, and `Resize` attempts without leaking `Vk*` types through RHI/renderer code. Current fail-closed paths populate `SkippedNotOperational`, `SkippedNoSwapchain`, `SkippedNoSwapchainImages`, and pending-resize statuses plus last frame/image indices, requested resize extent, availability booleans, last Vulkan result, and lifecycle counter values. CPU coverage: `VulkanFailClosedContract.FrameLifecycleDiagnosticsSnapshotReportsFailClosedStatuses`; opt-in `gpu;vulkan` coverage: `VulkanBootstrapSmoke.InitializeCreatesPerFrameResourcesOrFailsCleanly` asserts guarded bootstrap still leaves `BeginFrame` fail-closed with lifecycle diagnostics reflecting any registered swapchain availability.
  - Guarded promoted Vulkan bootstrap now makes real bring-up progress without changing the CPU/null path: with a native GLFW window, `VulkanDevice::Initialize()` initializes volk, creates a `VkInstance`, creates a window surface, enumerates physical devices, probes graphics/present/transfer queue families, requires swapchain extension/surface support plus Vulkan 1.2/1.3 features needed by later operational paths (sampled-image descriptor indexing update-after-bind/partially-bound, timeline semaphores, buffer device addresses, and dynamic rendering), creates a Vulkan logical device with `VK_KHR_swapchain` and those required features enabled, loads device-level volk entry points, acquires graphics/present/transfer `VkQueue` handles, creates a VMA allocator, allocates per-frame command pools, primary command buffers, fences, and acquire/render semaphores, then creates a backend-local swapchain with image views and `RHI::TextureHandle` registrations for swapchain images. `IsOperational()` still remains false until pipeline, bindless, transfer, presentation, resize, and device-loss reconciliation lands. `GetVulkanBootstrapDiagnosticsSnapshot()` exposes the most recent bootstrap attempt as backend-specific CPU diagnostics without leaking Vulkan-native types through RHI/renderer code, including logical-device creation, required-feature support/enablement, queue-acquisition, allocator-creation, per-frame resource counts, and swapchain image/view/handle counts plus extent. CPU coverage: `VulkanFailClosedContract.InitializeWithNullWindowSkipsBootstrapWithoutVulkanHandles`; opt-in `gpu;vulkan` coverage: `VulkanBootstrapSmoke.InitializeCreatesPerFrameResourcesOrFailsCleanly` validates that a GLFW/native-window host creates required-feature-enabled swapchain image/view/handle bootstrap state or fails/skips cleanly.
  - After successful swapchain image registration, guarded bootstrap now creates live internal `VulkanBindlessHeap`, global pipeline layout, `VulkanTransferQueue`/staging state, and rebinds per-frame command contexts to the live bindless descriptor set/layout. `GetVulkanServiceDiagnosticsSnapshot()` reports service handoff readiness or clean failure/skipped states. Public `GetBindlessHeap()` and `GetTransferQueue()` intentionally continue returning fail-closed fallback services while `IsOperational() == false`, so internal service creation does not silently expose operational resource traffic before pipeline/acquire/present/resize fallback reconciliation lands. Constructors for the internal services now leave invalid state plus logger diagnostics instead of aborting guarded bootstrap on allocation failures.   - Internal `VulkanTransferQueue` upload paths are hardened for future public handoff: command-buffer allocation/begin/end/submit and timeline semaphore query failures now log diagnostics and return invalid `RHI::TransferToken` values instead of aborting through `VK_CHECK_FATAL`; buffer/texture uploads preflight service validity, pool availability, source pointers/sizes, buffer ranges, mip/layer bounds, transfer-dst usage, and staging allocation before recording commands. ## Goal
- Wire concrete Vulkan backend execution behind the promoted graphics interfaces and default frame recipe.
## Non-goals
- No CPU-only contract behavior changes that require Vulkan.
- No mandatory GPU/Vulkan tests in the default CPU gate.
- No platform-window ownership move into renderer passes.
## Context
- Owner: `src/graphics/vulkan`, `src/graphics/rhi`, `src/graphics/framegraph`, and renderer backend wiring.
- CPU/null contracts should be stable before Vulkan execution is tightened.
## Required changes
- Connect Vulkan swapchain, descriptors, pipelines, barriers, transfer, and profiler support to canonical frame execution. Guarded instance/surface/physical-device probing, required Vulkan 1.2/1.3 feature negotiation, logical-device/queue/allocator/per-frame command/sync acquisition, swapchain image/view/handle registration, live internal service handoff, and direct SPIR-V pipeline creation are present and must remain fail-closed from renderer/RHI-manager execution until the remaining operational prerequisites land.
